The Challenge

Concord Servicing had built a strong business in loan and timeshare servicing, but the company's brand, products, and culture hadn't kept pace. The customer portal was outdated, the brand felt stale, and internal processes were slowing the team down. To compete and grow, the company needed a complete creative and product overhaul.

The Approach

I joined as Chief Creative Officer in September 2022 with a mandate to modernize everything the customer and employee touched. That meant brand, product, space, and culture, all at once.

  • Rebranded the entire company with a new visual identity that reflected where Concord was headed, not where it had been
  • Rebuilt the customer portal from the ground up, replacing a legacy experience with a modern, intuitive interface
  • Led the design of Link, Concord's flagship product, creating the experience layer for the platform
  • Led the design of Evault, a secure document management product
  • Redesigned the physical office space to reflect the new brand and support how people actually work
  • Modernized the working culture: shorter meetings, only necessary attendees, daily standups, and a bias toward shipping

Full Rebrand

The transformation touched every surface of the company. A new brand gave Concord a modern identity. A rebuilt portal gave customers a better experience. Link and Evault gave the sales team new products to sell. A redesigned office gave the team a space worth coming to. And a modernized culture made the whole organization faster and more focused.

Within 18 months of joining, the company was acquired, a direct reflection of the increased value created by the transformation. When a buyer looked at Concord, they saw a modern company with modern products, not a legacy business running on inertia.

What I Learned

Concord taught me that transformation isn't just about products or pixels. It's about changing how people see themselves and their company. A rebrand is a statement of intent. A redesigned office tells people their environment matters. Shorter meetings tell people their time matters. Each change reinforces the next.

It also reinforced something I've believed since Dialexa: speed is a feature. Eighteen months is fast for a transformation of this scope. We moved quickly because we didn't wait for permission, didn't over-process, and didn't let perfect kill good. Ship it, learn, improve.
