The Challenge

In 2014, most cars on the road had no connectivity. They generated enormous amounts of data (engine diagnostics, driving behavior, location) but none of it was accessible to developers, fleet operators, or the drivers themselves.

The automotive industry was moving slowly toward connected vehicles, but the existing solutions were fragmented, proprietary, and locked inside OEM ecosystems. There was no open platform that could turn any car into a connected car.

The Approach

Vinli started inside Dialexa Labs, our internal venture studio. We saw an opportunity to build the platform layer for the connected car. Not just a device, but an entire ecosystem.

  • Launched with an OBD-II device that could retrofit connectivity into any vehicle on the road
  • Built an open developer platform with APIs for vehicle data, enabling a thriving app ecosystem
  • Debuted at TechCrunch Disrupt Battlefield, putting the product in front of the entire tech industry
  • Evolved from consumer hardware into an enterprise vehicle data intelligence platform

Auto App Ecosystem

Vinli raised $21M across multiple rounds, backed by Samsung Global Innovation Center, Cox Automotive, Continental, and E.ON. Frost & Sullivan named it the largest app ecosystem in the automotive industry.

The company partnered with ALD Automotive across 42 countries and evolved into a mobility intelligence platform serving enterprise fleet and insurance clients. Today Vinli continues to operate, partnering with Toyota Data Solutions and Stellantis on commercial vehicle connectivity.

What I Learned

Vinli taught me how to incubate a venture from inside an existing company: how to give it enough independence to move fast while leveraging the parent organization's resources and talent. The venture studio model works when you treat the spin-out like a real startup, not a side project.

It also reinforced that the best products start with a platform thesis. We didn't just build a connected car device. We built the ecosystem around it. The device was the wedge; the platform was the product.
