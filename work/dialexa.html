Role Co-founder
Timeline 2010 — 2022
Outcome Acquired by IBM

The Challenge

Enterprise companies needed a technology partner who could think beyond implementation. They had ideas, budgets, and urgency—but lacked the design-engineering integration to build products that actually worked for real users.

The consultancy landscape was fragmented: design shops that couldn't build, dev shops that couldn't design, and legacy firms that moved too slowly. There was a gap for a company that combined deep technical capability with product design thinking.

The Approach

We built Dialexa around a single conviction: great products require design and engineering to be inseparable. Not sequential. Not siloed. Integrated from day one.

  • Cross-functional teams where designers and engineers worked side by side from kickoff to launch
  • A product-led methodology that prioritized user outcomes over feature checklists
  • Deep technical bench across IoT, cloud infrastructure, mobile, data, and AI
  • A culture of builders—people who shipped, not people who presented

Years Building

Dialexa grew from a two-person startup in Dallas to a 300+ person technology firm working with enterprise clients across healthcare, finance, automotive, and energy. We shipped hundreds of products, built a reputation for technical excellence, and created a culture that attracted exceptional talent.

In 2022, IBM acquired Dialexa to strengthen IBM Consulting's technology capabilities—validating the thesis that design-led engineering creates outsized value.

What I Learned

Building Dialexa taught me that the best products come from teams that refuse to separate thinking from making. Strategy without execution is a slide deck. Execution without strategy is a feature factory. The magic is in the integration.

It also taught me that company culture isn't a mission statement—it's the thousand small decisions about who you hire, how you run meetings, and what you celebrate. We built a place where people loved coming to work, and that's what made everything else possible.
